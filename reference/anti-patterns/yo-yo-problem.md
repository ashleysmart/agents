# Yo-yo Problem

A deep inheritance hierarchy where understanding a method requires jumping up and down through many ancestor classes.

**Applies to**: Object-Oriented Design

---

**What it is**: A deep inheritance hierarchy where understanding a method
requires jumping up and down through many ancestor classes.

**Why not**: The reader cannot understand a class in isolation. Behaviour
is scattered across ancestors in ways that are hard to trace and easy to
break with a change at any level.
